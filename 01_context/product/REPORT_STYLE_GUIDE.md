# GrahAI Report Style Guide

> A report is not a data dump. It's the user's life story told through one lens.

---

## Report Philosophy

Every GrahAI report is a chapter in the user's life story. It should feel like sitting down with someone who deeply understands your chart and is explaining what it means for this specific area of your life — clearly, honestly, with references you can trace, and guidance you can act on.

A report is NOT:
- A textbook excerpt about planetary principles
- A list of chart factors without interpretation
- A generic self-help article with astrology keywords sprinkled in
- A fear-based warning system
- An incomprehensible wall of Sanskrit terms

A report IS:
- Personal (written for one chart, one person)
- Narrative (tells a story, not lists facts)
- Source-aware (every claim is traceable)
- Practical (ends with something useful)
- Honest (challenges are named, not hidden)

---

## Universal Report Structure

Every report follows this structure, regardless of type:

### 1. Report Summary (Required)
**Length:** 3-5 sentences
**Purpose:** The essential insight. What the user should take away even if they read nothing else.
**Must include:**
- User's ascendant and the primary chart factor for this report's topic
- Current dasha context (what chapter they're in)
- The single most important insight
- A grounding statement about what to do with this information

**Example (Career Blueprint):**
> "As a Sagittarius rising with Mercury ruling your 10th house, your career path favors communication, analysis, and intellectual work. Mercury's retrograde status in your 1st house gives you a revisionary, detail-oriented working style that improves with experience. You're currently in Mars Mahadasha — a chapter of action and assertion that's pushing you toward independence and leadership. The most important thing to understand: your career isn't about finding the perfect role, it's about building one that matches your analytical nature and growing autonomy."

### 2. Main Analysis Sections (3-6 sections per report)
**Length:** 400-700 words per section
**Purpose:** Deep, personalized analysis of each dimension of the topic.

**Each section must follow:**

```
[Section Title — Clear, Topic-Specific]

[Opening: 1-2 sentences naming the primary chart factor for this section]

[Body: 3-4 paragraphs]
  Paragraph 1: Primary factor — planet + sign + house + dignity + what it means
  Paragraph 2: Supporting factor — aspect, yoga, or secondary planet + how it modifies the picture
  Paragraph 3: Timing context — current dasha/transit influence on this dimension
  Paragraph 4: Story connection — how this dimension fits the user's larger chart narrative

[Based On box: 2-3 chart factors listed with strength indicators]

[Practical Guidance: 2-3 actionable points]
  - What to lean into
  - What to watch out for
  - When to act (timing window if applicable)
```

### 3. Timing & Forecast Section (Required for timing-relevant reports)
**Length:** 300-500 words
**Purpose:** When things intensify, ease, or change.
**Must include:**
- Current Mahadasha/Antardasha and what they activate
- Next 12-month transit outlook for the report's topic area
- Specific windows of opportunity or caution
- When the current pattern shifts

### 4. Remedies & Guidance Section (Required)
**Length:** 300-500 words
**Purpose:** What the user can do with this knowledge.
**Must include:**
- Remedies specific to the user's chart imbalance (not generic lists)
- Why this remedy matches this specific chart pattern
- How long to maintain the practice
- What changes to look for
- Behavioral or practical suggestions (not just ritual)

**Remedy format:**
```
[Remedy Name]
Why: [Which chart factor this addresses]
Practice: [What to do]
Duration: [How long]
What to watch for: [Expected changes or signs of effect]
```

### 5. Methodology & Sources Appendix (Required)
**Length:** 150-300 words
**Purpose:** Transparency about what was analyzed.
**Must include:**
- Charts consulted (Rashi, Navamsa, Dasamsa, etc.)
- Systems used (Vimshottari Dasha, Ashtakavarga, Shadbala)
- Number of factors considered
- Classical texts referenced with chapter numbers
- Any limitations or caveats

## Writing Rules for Reports

### Opening Every Paragraph
Every analysis paragraph must open with a chart-specific reference. Never open with a general principle.

**Wrong:** "The 7th house in Vedic astrology represents partnerships and marriage."
**Right:** "Your 7th house is ruled by Mercury and falls in Gemini — partnerships for you are built on communication, intellectual exchange, and curiosity."

### Dignity-Driven Language

Planet dignity must shape the entire tone of interpretation:

| Dignity | Language Pattern | Emotional Tone |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Exalted | "One of its most powerful placements..." / "Naturally strong..." | Confident, expansive |
| Own Sign | "Comfortable and authentic..." / "Expressing naturally..." | Grounded, assured |
| Friendly | "Well-supported..." / "Good conditions for..." | Positive, steady |
| Neutral | "Functional but requiring effort..." / "Steady, not dramatic..." | Balanced, practical |
| Enemy | "Working against its nature..." / "Requires conscious adjustment..." | Honest, constructive |
| Debilitated | "Challenged..." / "Your growth edge..." / "Not impossible, but earned..." | Compassionate, empowering |

**Never:** Frame debilitation as doom. Always frame it as growth that requires more effort.

### Nakshatra Integration

When a nakshatra is relevant, weave it into the personality description:

**Pattern:** "[Planet] is in [Nakshatra] — the nakshatra of [one-line quality]. This adds a [quality] dimension to [life area being discussed]."

**Example:** "Your Venus is in Rohini nakshatra — the nakshatra of beauty, fertility, and material abundance. This adds a sensual, aesthetically attuned dimension to how you experience love."

### Yoga Integration

When a yoga is present and relevant to the report's topic:

**Pattern:** "[Yoga Name] is active in your chart — a combination of [planets involved] that gives [effect]. In the context of [report topic], this means [specific interpretation]."

**Example:** "Budha-Aditya Yoga is active in your chart — a combination of Sun and Mercury in the same house that gives sharp intellect and communicative authority. In the context of your career, this means you naturally lead through ideas and clarity rather than force."

### Dosha Handling

Doshas must be handled honestly but constructively:

1. Name the dosha clearly
2. Explain what it means in plain language
3. Explain what it does NOT mean (counter common misconceptions)
4. Explain cancellation factors if any exist in the chart
5. Provide specific remedies

**Example:**
> "Your chart shows Kuja Dosha — Mars placed in your 7th house. In plain terms, this means Mars's energy of assertion and friction touches your partnership space. This does NOT mean marriage is impossible or doomed — that's a common misconception. In your case, Mars is in a friendly sign, which moderates the effect. What it does mean: you may attract partners who are strong-willed, or experience periods of friction in relationships that require direct communication. The remedy is specific to your chart: Mars is your 5th house lord, so creative expression and physical activity are natural outlets that reduce relational friction."

## Report-Specific Guidelines

### Career Blueprint
- Lead with 10th house lord analysis
- Include D10 (Dasamsa) insights for career depth
- Mention specific career archetypes matched to chart factors (not generic "you could be a leader")
- Include timing for career moves (dasha-based windows)
- Practical: industry alignment, working style, when to change roles

### Love & Compatibility
- Lead with Venus analysis (sign, house, dignity, nakshatra)
- Include Moon for emotional compatibility style
- Include 7th house for partnership dynamics
- Include D9 (Navamsa) for deeper commitment potential
- Practical: what to seek in a partner, relationship growth areas, timing for commitment

### Annual Forecast
- Structure by quarters or months
- Each period: dominant transit + dasha interaction + what it means
- Include highlight months (strongest positive, most challenging)
- Practical: month-by-month priorities and focus areas

### Kundli Match
- Full Ashtakoot breakdown (all 8 components)
- Synastry: how each person's chart interacts with the other's
- Dosha comparison (especially Kuja Dosha)
- Practical: where to invest in the relationship, where to establish healthy boundaries

### Marriage Timing
- Dasha-based windows (when 7th lord is activated)
- Transit-based windows (Jupiter and Venus through relationship houses)
- Include Navamsa timing indicators
- Practical: best windows in next 2-3 years, what to prepare before committing

### Wealth & Growth
- 2nd house (earned income) + 11th house (gains) analysis
- Jupiter and Venus for wealth indicators
- Dhana yogas if present
- Practical: financial decision timing, investment style matched to chart

### Dasha Deep Dive
- Current Mahadasha + all remaining Antardashas listed with themes
- Pratyantardasha for next 12 months
- Life phase narrative across the full Mahadasha period
- Practical: what each sub-period brings and how to prepare

## Uniqueness Standards

| Metric | Target | Method |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Overall chart-specific content | >65% unique | Bigram cross-profile comparison |
| Generic sentences per section | <3 | Manual audit |
| Chart factors per section | ≥2 | Code audit of generators |
| Sections without user's name or chart reference | 0 | Automated check |

**Rule:** If a section can be removed from the report and reassigned to a different user's chart without editing, it fails.
